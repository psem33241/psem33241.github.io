NETFIX : Python & Streamlit Project

November 2024 - January 2025

Welcome to our exciting project to create a movie recommendation system for a local cinema in Creuse, France, which aspires to integrate modern digital features to attract and retain customers. This project was carried out as part of my data analyst training at Wild Code School.

Introduction

Our mission was to develop a movie recommendation engine, similar to that of Netflix, but adapted to the specific needs of our client. This ambitious project aims to offer viewers a personalized experience, by suggesting films they might enjoy, based on their preferences and behaviors.

Objective & Challenges

In a ‚Äúcold start‚Äù context where no preferences had yet been filled in by customers, we exploited a database of films from IMDb to get started. We conducted detailed market research into cinema consumption in the Creuse region, and carried out in-depth analysis of the data to extract key trends and characteristics.

  • Trends analysis : Identification of the most popular actors, evolution of the average length of films, comparison between film and TV actors, average age of actors, and characteristics of top-rated films.
  • Customized recommendations : Use of machine learning algorithms to suggest films based on viewer preferences.

Tools & Resources

The data used comes from IMDb and TMDB, covering various aspects such as actors, directors, scores, budget, receipts, etc. We also retrieved movie posters for an enriched user experience. Analysis and visualization were performed using tools such as Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Scikit-learn.
