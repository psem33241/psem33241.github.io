GEOLITHE : Assistant Geophysicist

From May 2018 to February 2019

From May 2018 to February 2019, I had the opportunity to work for 6 months as a geophysical intern as part of my final year project as well as a further two months on a fixed-term contract as an assistant geophysical engineer at Geolithe in Crolles, France. My main role was to acquire and process subsurface geophysical data, contributing to geological and environmental analysis projects.

Key responsibilities :

  • Shallow Geophysical Data Acquisition : I have been involved in subsurface geophysical data acquisition, using techniques such as resistivity tomography, seismic refraction and microgravimetry.

  • Data processing : I used intelligence software to process data, interpret results and write mission reports, ensuring clear communication of the results obtained.

  • Drafting and archiving acquisition programs : I was also responsible for writing and archiving acquisition programs, ensuring that all procedures were documented and accessible for future projects.

This experience enabled me to strengthen my skills in geophysical data acquisition and processing, as well as in scientific communication, while working in a collaborative and dynamic environment.
